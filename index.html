BFA 2021

Breakout Room

Zoom lingo grossly pervades our classroom sensory experiences. We can’t imagine seeing each other without also noticing tilted posters, the abundant (lack of) natural light in peoples’ bedrooms, or a garden of grey and red ‘mutes’—for when we didn’t feel like it. These glimpses into our peers’ private lives blur boundaries for our formal spaces, but also cue us into learning opportunities that bloom in new liminal spaces.

Enter our Breakout Room. Stacked 2, 3, 5, or 8 ways, we’d ease into a familiar critique process. Our breakout rooms chip away at the main lobby’s hierarchical grid, and allow us to intimate a more true hybrid classroom.

Donning our exhibition with screen-friendly colors might feel like a retreat into our isolated, digital modules. Are we wasting a rare opportunity to move away from cyber-fatigue? However, we as designers make homes in the iterative and imaginative. Our class selected this chroma green & projector blue for our glaring perseverance for celebrating classmates’ innovative work and ‘projecting’ community. The ubiquitous green and blue we also find in our skies and fields echo the physical experience for breaking outside of our Zoom boxes for outdoor inspiration. Unique pixel glyphs—one for each student—illustrate our respective creative experiences and comforts, and tether our work to the at-large BFA Thesis Show identity.

Our thesis group show—with Lego–like organization—represents all the relentless relationships we’ve been able to link and revisit in the past year. When the 33 BFA Graphic Design students graduate this 2021, we’ll realize that, all this time, breaking out meant shining together.
